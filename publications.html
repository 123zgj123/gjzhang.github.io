Hallucination as Exploit: Evidence-Carrying Multimodal Agents
Guijia Zhang, et al.
Preprint, 2026. arXiv:2605.19192.
arXiv PDF
Formalizes hallucination-to-action conversion, where unsupported perceptual claims authorize privileged actions, and proposes evidence-carrying agents that decompose tool calls into action-critical predicates certified by constrained DOM/OCR/AX verifiers, with a deterministic authorization gate.
[2]
STARS: Skill-Triggered Audit for Request-Conditioned Invocation Safety in Agent Systems
Guijia Zhang, et al.
Preprint, 2026. arXiv:2604.10286.
arXiv PDF
Formulates invocation-time auditing of agent skill calls, scoring risk from the user request, candidate skill, and runtime context, and introduces SIA-Bench, a 3,000-record benchmark with leakage-free source-group splits and a held-out indirect-prompt-injection split.
[3]
Agent Skill Regulation as a Procedural Knowledge Representation Problem
Co-first author: Guijia Zhang, with collaborators at HKUST
Preprint, 2026.
Reframes agent skill regulation as a procedural knowledge representation problem, modeling execution commitments, state constraints, and recovery actions across syntax, semantics, and execution levels, with bidirectional optimization between formal prior libraries and procedural evidence in human-written skills.
[4]
Do GUI Agents Believe Their Eyes? Diagnosing State-Belief Reliance on Pixels versus Structure
Guijia Zhang, et al.
Preprint, 2026. arXiv:2607.04334.
arXiv PDF
Formalizes visual state reliance in multimodal GUI agents and measures it with paired single-channel interventions over 310 real probes, introducing the Perception-Fusion Gap to quantify how textual state beliefs defer to serialized structure even when pixel-level perception is nearly perfect.
